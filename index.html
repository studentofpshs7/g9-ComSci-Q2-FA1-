Morning Routine

I wake up at 5 am to get to the field early for work. I get out of bed, then I take a quick shower, to save time. After my shower, I change to some clothes that cover me well, so I don't get sunburnt later in the day. I make a sandwich for breakfast then I leave the house at 7am.

Today I have to catch some insect specimens, so I am now on my way to a forest in the area. While waiting during ride there and eating my sandwich, I check what insects I need to get.

Midday Tasks & Projects

I make it to the forest, and eventually find a good spot with a couple bugs I need. I set down my tools & equipment, and take out my net. First I need some flying insects, so I attempt to catch some of them with my net, the ones I caught being put in a killing jar. These jars work like a mini gas chamber to kill insects more conveniently. It's especially helpful since insects are rather difficult to kill without accidently squashing them.

After a few hours of that, I eat my lunch. Afterwards, the work continues; the rest of my tasks are noting the bugs that can be found in the area, and making sure some invasive bug species are being kept in check. Later I will compile my notes and other information in a document when I get back home.

A Story from My Dream Job

Insects are very useful and can even help in a crime scene, for "they never lie". They can leave lots pf traces, and can help you just by doing what they do.

An example applying this include, insects like coffin flies. They dig about 2 meters in the ground to breed in near or in human corpses. Using these bugs, you can find out how long a corpse has been buried for, -which can help to verify and piece evidence together in solving a case.
